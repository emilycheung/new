Geography of China

China is the world's third largest country, with an area of approximately 9.6 million square kilometers. It has a coastline of 18,000 kilometers and is bordered by 14 countries. The vast land expanses of China consists of plains, basins, plateaus, mountains and foothills. The highest step of the topography is made up by the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau with an average height of over 4,000 meters, including the Kunlunshan range, Qilianshan range and Hengduan mountain chain. Lower mountains and foothills contain altitudes of over 500 meters. From the higher elevations in the west thousands of rivers drain the country; the most significant include the Yangtze (third longest river on the planet), and the Heilong (Amur), Mekong, Pearl and Yellow.
